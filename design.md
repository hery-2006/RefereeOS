# RefereeOS Design Direction

## Theme

RefereeOS should feel like an NYC civic operations desk overlooking Central Park: structured, credible, high-signal, and calm under pressure. The interface should remain a working reviewer dashboard, not a decorative tourism page.

## Visual Principles

- **Central Park as system map:** use park green, reservoir blue, limestone, asphalt, ironwork black, and small taxi-yellow accents.
- **Operational density:** preserve the evidence-board workflow and make scan paths clear for judges under time pressure.
- **Civic confidence:** panels should feel like durable public-infrastructure signage and control-room surfaces.
- **Restrained atmosphere:** use subtle street-grid and park-map textures only in page/background surfaces; do not obscure content.
- **Premium trust over AI novelty:** use frosted-material controls sparingly, with grounded shadows and crisp focus states instead of neon glow.

- Paper white: `#fffdf7`
- Asphalt: `#17201d`
- Iron: `#26312d`
- Taxi accent: `#f2c14e`
- Brick alert: `#9f3a2f`

## Component Notes

- Header: dark park-green civic band with subtle map-grid texture.
- Panels: limestone/paper glass surfaces with 6px radius, darker borders, and compact headings.
- Buttons: restrained glassmorphic material, visible borders, clear hover/focus states, and no decorative glow.
- Primary action: taxi-yellow glass button with asphalt text for instant demo visibility.
- Repro passed state: park green.
- Repro failed/high-risk state: brick red.
- Medium-risk state: taxi-yellow/brown.
- Evidence rows: left color rule stays as the main scan affordance.

## Content Tone

Use professional, reviewer-facing language. Avoid novelty language like "AI magic," avoid publication accept/reject claims, and keep the ethical boundary visible in generated packets.
